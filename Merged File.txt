Seventeen is a South Korean boy band formed by Pledis Entertainment.
The group consists of thirteen members: S.Coups, Jeonghan, Joshua, Jun, Hoshi, Wonwoo, Woozi, DK, Mingyu, The8, Seungkwan, Vernon, and Dino.
Throughout their career, they have reached a large audience and grown into an internationally recognized K-pop group with signature music and performances.
DK, whose real name is Lee Seokmin, is a South Korean singer and a member of the popular K-pop boy group SEVENTEEN.
Born on February 18, 1997, he is best known as the group’s main vocalist and is part of the Vocal Unit and the fun, high-energy sub-unit BSS (BooSeokSoon), alongside Seungkwan and Hoshi.
DK is widely loved for his powerful and emotional vocals, bright personality, and infectious laughter that lifts the mood of everyone around him.
Often referred to as SEVENTEEN’s "sunlight," his stage presence is both captivating and heartfelt, especially during high-note moments in songs like “Don’t Wanna Cry” and “Thanks".
Beyond his idol career, DK has also shown his versatility by performing in musical theater, notably in the Korean production of Xcalibur.
Fans admire his humility, dedication, and ability to make people smile, making him one of the most cherished members of the group.
